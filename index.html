FAQ

Q: Should we be focusing our attention on other world problems? Space is something that you do because it is interesting and fun not to help humanity.

A: Increasing the size of space programs around the world has the potential to usher in a new era of science and technology. If we as a species want to increase the pace of technological strides then space programs are one of the best things you can put your money in. Just look at what the space programs of the 60s did for global research.

Q: Why Mars? It‚Äôs not a hospitable planet for humans.

A: Mars is a fantastic place to start humanity's great exploration of the solar system. Basically due to distance, atmospheric density and gravity, Mars is a great place to start and once we have conquered Mars we can conquer the rest of the solar system. The lessons we learn while attempting to reach Mars will be vital and might allow us to start independent economic missions that trade resources with other planets and also missions with the goal of mining asteroids. As for the dangers, learning to survive on Mars and creating a secure life of relative luxury will be a great stepping stone for the colonization of the rest of the solar system. There are far more dangerous planets waiting to be exploited and if we can survive Mars, we can survive anything.

Q: Why now?

A: The interest in space is at an all time high measured from the 2000s and as global conflicts plague the world, a mission to Mars could unify us all. It will give the nations of the world a peaceful goal to all work towards while also providing a potential economic benefit in 30 years if mineral exports become economically viable.

Q: Where will the money come from?

A: The funding of these space programs would hopefully come from a budget reduction of national militaries. As a species we are wasting so much money and resources on hostility when we can look past our differences and focus on a common goal that will unify and positively affect all of humanity.
